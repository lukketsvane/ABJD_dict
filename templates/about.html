ABJD Dictionary

Project Overview

The ABJD Dictionary is a speculative exploration of Semitic languages and phonosemantics, inspired by the ancient ABJD system. This project aims to create a unique dictionary that maps the intrinsic power and meanings of Semitic sounds.

Core Concepts

Semitic Linguistic Sounds

We investigate how Semitic linguistic sounds form an algorithm that predicts letter combinations for specific concepts. This research delves into whether concepts determine the letters used to describe them, or if letters predate concepts in time.

Emergence in Linguistics

The project focuses on the phenomenon of "emergence" in linguistic systems. We explore how combining certain entities can lead to the emergence of patterns or systems, regardless of their chaotic or organized nature.

Key Features

  • Triliteral Root System: Explores the three-letter root system fundamental to Semitic languages.
  • Letter-Sound Associations: Examines the relationships between letters, their sounds, and associated meanings.
  • Numerical Values: Investigates the numerical aspects of letters and their potential significance.
  • Environmental Influence: Considers how the original environment of Semitic languages influenced their development.

Theoretical Framework

  • Phonosemantic Associations: Each letter carries specific connotations, shapes, colors, and phenomena.
  • Cultural and Historical Context: Incorporates research on Safaitic epigraphs and other historical linguistic sources.
  • Cross-Disciplinary Approach: Integrates concepts from linguistics, philosophy, and cultural studies.

Future Directions

  1. Timeline of sound chronology in Semitic languages
  2. Exploration of vocality and tonality in relation to climate and environment
  3. Development of a comprehensive algorithm for predicting letter-concept relationships

Artistic and Academic Applications

  • Creation of sine wave prints for vocalized letters
  • Compilation of a booklet on sound meanings and associations
  • Potential for an immersive exhibition engaging all five senses

Conclusion

The ABJD Dictionary project offers a unique lens through which to view language, combining historical linguistic analysis with speculative future-oriented thinking. It seeks to uncover hidden connections between sounds, symbols, and meanings in Semitic languages, potentially revealing new insights into the nature of language and human cognition.
